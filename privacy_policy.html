Privacy Policy

Fraud Detection Alerts Program

Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect personal information as part of the Fraud Detection Alerts Program. This is an educational project and not a production financial service.

Information We Collect

We collect the following information from users:

  • Phone number (for SMS communication)
  • Account identifier (internal use only)
  • Transaction data submitted during program use
  • User responses sent via SMS (e.g., YES/NO)

How We Use Your Information

Your information is used only for the purposes of:

  • Sending fraud detection alerts and verification messages
  • Processing your SMS responses
  • Improving fraud classification accuracy
  • Maintaining system functionality

How We Store and Protect Your Data

All data is stored in secure cloud services within AWS (Amazon Web Services). Access is restricted to authorized system components only. No personal data is shared with third parties except our SMS provider (Twilio) for message delivery.

Third-Party Services

We use Twilio to send and receive SMS messages. Twilio may store message logs for compliance and delivery tracking. Twilio's privacy policy applies to SMS routing and processing.

Opt-Out Policy

You may stop receiving messages at any time by replying:

  • STOP – to unsubscribe from all alerts
  • HELP – to receive help information

Data Retention

User data and SMS logs may be retained for project evaluation and debugging during the academic semester. Data may be deleted at the end of the project.

Educational Purpose Notice

This system is a student project built for the Capital One Capstone Program and is not intended for real-world financial use. No real credit card numbers or banking data should be submitted.
